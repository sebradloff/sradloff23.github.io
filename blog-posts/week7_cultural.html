Cultural Blog, Week 7

What do I value?

When I think of the times in my life where I've been the happiest, the proudest, or the most satisfied, the following values come to mind: accomplishment, ambition, competition, excellence, personal development, physical challenge, and recognition.

I find myself valuing the accomplishment of difficult mental or physical tasks. That feeling once you finish an incredibly arduous task, whether it's a personal record on my squat or solving a difficult Ruby coding exercise, results in both a giant sense relief and an almost perpetual state of awesomeness. This feeling allows me to "win the day", meaning no matter how bad of a day I had before this moment, everything is now alright and will be alright. "Winning the day" clears my mind of negative thoughts and feelings, allowing me to focus on the good and the great.

From the values I listed earlier, I can personally say that I do not necessarily live up to the values. These values are instead so deeply ingrained into who I am that I simply live them. I have a need to consistently improve both my mental and physical strength, that all my values are byproducts.

The last piece of advice I gave was to a friend who was choosing between graduate school or a job. I try to avoid giving people advice, unless I have specifically been in their situation, instead I act as a good listener and make both pro and con arguments that they may or may not have thought of before.

How do I feel when I think about my values? I guess I think that somewhere in there explains why I'm such a motivated and dedicated person when it comes to any engaging task put in front of me.

If I'm ever feeling down at DBC, I think I'll take a second to remember how far I've come, how much I've learned, how much I've grown, to be in this moment learning from a supportive community. Then I'll realize how much longer the road ahead of me is, but how manageable each stretch on the long road can become with the right mindset. That's what I'll think about.
