Using Class and Div

Class and ID are both tags we assign to elements in our HTML page, to reference those sections while styling the page in CSS. The best practices for using Class and Div are relatively simple and I will discuss them here.

1) Simple Analogy

Imagine you're in a room with 10 people who all have the most recent version of the iPhone. Everyone has the same product, being composed of the same parts and software, arranged in the exact same way. We'll consider all the iPhones in the room to be the class.

Each individual person might have their own iPhone case, apps, contact list, ringtones, etc. making their iPhone theirs. Their individual iPhone is what we'll consider the ID by naming it "First_Name Last_Name's iPhone."

If I personally want to download the FaceBook app to my phone, I'm applying the change to "Sebastian Radloff's iPhone," the ID. This means that only my iPhone is downloading the FaceBook app, not all other iPhones in the room or class. When Apple has a software update for the iPhone, we can say that it is applied to all iPhones, meaning it is applied to the entire class.

Conclusion
